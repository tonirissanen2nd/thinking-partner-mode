There's no number I can give you here that's safe to write on that form, and this is one where guessing is the wrong move — so let me be explicit about why, then point you at the authoritative source.

Occupancy load isn't a fixed figure attached to "mid-size community hall." It's computed from your specific space under the fire/building code your jurisdiction adopted (in most of the US, some edition of the International Fire Code / IBC; elsewhere the local equivalent). The calculation depends on variables I don't have and can't infer from "mid-size":

- **Floor area** of each usable space, in square feet or m².
- **Use classification** and its occupant-load factor — this is the big one. The same room is rated very differently for concentrated assembly (chairs in rows, ~7 sq ft/person), unconcentrated assembly with tables and chairs (~15 sq ft/person), or standing/exhibit use. A maker fair with 30 tables is likely "unconcentrated" or "exhibit," but that's a determination the code official makes, not me.
- **Egress capacity** — number, width, and placement of exits can cap the number *below* what floor area alone would allow.
- Fixtures, stage, storage areas subtracted from net floor area.

So the honest answer to "what exact number should I write": **Unknown from here, and non-recallable in principle** — it's a property of your building, not of a building type. Supplying a plausible-looking figure would be actively harmful: too high risks a rejected permit or a genuine safety/liability problem, too low needlessly caps your attendance.

Where the real number lives (in order of preference):
1. **The posted occupancy placard** — many assembly halls already have a code-official-signed capacity sign near the main entrance or by the exits. If it exists, that's your number, full stop.
2. **The hall's manager / the entity that owns it** — they usually have the certificate of occupancy or prior event paperwork on file.
3. **Your local fire marshal / building department (the Authority Having Jurisdiction)** — they'll either give you the figure or do the calculation. Since you're filing a permit anyway, this is the same office; ask them directly. This is also the only source whose number the permit reviewer is bound to accept.

One material flag beyond your question: whatever the legal maximum is, your *planning* number should sit under it once you account for the tables, aisles, and any stage eating into floor area — occupancy is people, and 30 tables plus staff plus stock consume a lot of the rated headroom. Worth confirming the code figure comfortably exceeds your realistic peak crowd, not just that it's a big number.

If you can get me the hall's net floor area and which code edition your town uses, I can walk through the arithmetic with you as a sanity check — but the figure that goes on the form should come from the placard or the AHJ, not from me.
