# Forecasts — Thinking Partner Mode

Note on scope: the question list contains items 1–13 (the "1–15" in the closing instruction has no items 14–15 in the prompt). I forecast every item that exists. Each forecast is stated as a scorable claim with a reference class, a base-rate anchor, and named departures from it. I stand at the start of each resolution window and use only prior knowledge.

## 1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)?

Reference class: official (record-eligible, IAAF/World Athletics-legal) men's marathon world records. The record has ticked down in ~30–90 second steps roughly every 1–3 years (Kipchoge 2:01:39 in 2018, 2:01:09 in 2022; Kiptum 2:00:35 in Chicago 2023). The sub-2:00 barrier under record-legal conditions is a large discrete jump: the standing record is 2:00:35, so ~36 seconds must come off in one or two more record cycles.

Outside view: barrier-breaking in one specified ~2-year window is uncommon; record cycles are lumpy. Inside-view adjustments: (a) Kiptum, the athlete most likely to do it, died in early 2024 — a major negative; (b) the 1:59:40 in Vienna (2019) was a non-legal exhibition, showing the physiological ceiling is near but not that conditions/pacing convert easily to legal races; (c) super-shoe gains are largely priced in already. The remaining gap plus loss of the front-runner makes a *legal, official* sub-2:00 in this specific window unlikely.

- Confidence: moderate-high, basis = size of remaining gap + Kiptum's death removing the leading candidate.

PROB 1: 3

## 2. P(Python 3.14.0 stable is released before mid-2026)?

Reference class: CPython's fixed annual cadence. Since 3.9, the final release ships on a highly reliable schedule — a feature freeze (beta) in spring, release candidates late summer, and the **.0 final in early October**. 3.12 → Oct 2023, 3.13 → Oct 2024. So 3.14.0 final is scheduled for **October 2025**, comfortably before mid-2026.

Base rate for CPython hitting its planned .0 within a ~9-month margin: extremely high; slips are measured in days, not quarters. Only a black-swan project disruption defeats this, and the process is distributed/robust.

- Confidence: very high, basis = deterministic PEP release calendar with long, reliable track record.

PROB 2: 98

## 3. P(Nvidia becomes the world's most valuable company at some point during 2025)?

Reference class: mega-cap market-cap leadership. Entering 2025, Nvidia had already, during 2024, repeatedly traded as the world's most valuable company (briefly overtaking Apple/Microsoft in June 2024 and again around late 2024), driven by the AI/datacenter capex cycle. The question only requires it to hold the #1 spot at *any point* during 2025 — a very low bar given it was already oscillating at the top with Apple and Microsoft.

Outside view (a company already at/near #1 touches #1 at least once over a full year): very high. Inside view: AI-capex momentum was still strong at the start of 2025; a full-year drought where it never once leads would require a sustained relative collapse. Low probability of that.

- Confidence: high, basis = starting position already at the top of the leaderboard + full-year "any point" criterion.

PROB 3: 95

## 4. P(Max Verstappen wins the 2025 Formula 1 World Drivers' Championship)?

Reference class: reigning multi-title champion in a stable-regulation year. Verstappen won 2021–2024 (four straight). But 2025 is the last year before the 2026 regulation reset, and the 2024 season already showed Red Bull's car advantage eroding late-season while McLaren (and Ferrari/Norris/Piastri) closed the gap; McLaren took the 2024 constructors' title.

Outside view: base rate that the dominant recent champion repeats is high but not overwhelming when a rival team has demonstrably reached parity. Decompose: P(Red Bull car is championship-competitive) × P(Verstappen beats teammate and rival lead driver given a competitive car). The first factor is the swing risk — McLaren looked like the car to beat entering 2025. I put Verstappen clearly in contention but no longer the odds-on favorite; roughly a coin-flip-to-slightly-below given a strong multi-team field.

- Confidence: low-moderate, basis = driver skill edge offset by real risk of an inferior car.

PROB 4: 40

## 7. P(the US Federal Reserve cuts its policy rate at least once during 2025)?

Reference class: FOMC policy over a full calendar year. The Fed began an easing cycle in September 2024 (50bp) and cut further into late 2024, with the policy rate elevated relative to a normalizing inflation path. Entering 2025, the modal path (dot plot / market pricing) contemplated additional cuts, even if fewer than markets once hoped.

The bar is only *one* cut across the whole year, with eight meetings. For zero cuts to occur, inflation would need to reaccelerate sharply and persistently enough to keep the Fed on hold or hiking for all of 2025 — possible but not the central case given the disinflation trend and a still-cooling labor market. Given eight opportunities and an easing bias, at least one cut is very likely.

- Confidence: high, basis = active easing cycle + low "at least one" bar over eight meetings.

PROB 7: 90

## 8. P(Bitcoin trades above $150,000 at any point during 2025)?

Reference class: BTC bull-cycle peaks in post-halving years. The April 2024 halving historically precedes a blow-off top 12–18 months later; BTC set an all-time high above ~$100k in late 2024 following spot-ETF inflows and a favorable US political/regulatory shift. So $150k is roughly a further ~50% from the late-2024 highs.

Decompose: P(a 2025 cycle top occurs) is high, but P(that top exceeds $150k specifically) is the swing. Prior cycles ran multiples above the prior ATH, which argues for reaching $150k; but ETF-era cycles may be more muted, and a full-year "any point" criterion helps. Net: better than even but genuinely uncertain — the exact level is where the fat tail of crypto price makes point estimates shaky, though $150k is within a plausible cycle-top band rather than a true black swan.

- Confidence: low-moderate, basis = post-halving cycle mechanics vs. uncertainty about peak magnitude.

PROB 8: 55

## 9. P(the Kansas City Chiefs win Super Bowl LX (Feb 2026))?

Reference class: single-team probability of winning a given Super Bowl. The unconditional base rate is 1/32 ≈ 3%. The Chiefs are a strong, repeat contender (three titles in the Mahomes era, back-to-back entering the 2025 season), so their preseason title odds sit well above base — typically top-of-market, in the ~15–20% range for the best team.

Outside view corrects the temptation to overweight recency: even the best team rarely exceeds ~20% to win it all before a season starts, because a 14-game/single-elimination gauntlet has enormous variance and injury risk (esp. QB). I anchor near elite-favorite odds.

- Confidence: moderate, basis = strong-favorite market odds bounded by single-elimination variance.

## 10. P(SpaceX Starship completes a successful in-space payload deployment before 2026)?

Reference class: Starship integrated test-flight progression through 2024–2025. By late 2024 SpaceX had achieved ascent, a booster catch, and controlled reentry, and a Starlink-simulator payload-deploy demo was on the near-term flight manifest. But the deploy attempt slipped; and the early-2025 flights (Flight 7/8) suffered upper-stage failures (Ship losses), pushing a clean payload-deployment demonstration to the right.

Decompose: P(a flight carrying a deploy demo occurs before 2026) × P(that flight succeeds through deployment). The program's rapid iteration argues for eventual success, but the recent run of Ship-stage failures plus the specific "successful deployment" bar within this window makes a clean success uncertain. Roughly a coin flip, tilted by the demonstrated failure streak.

- Confidence: low-moderate, basis = capability plausibly there but recent upper-stage failures + narrow window.

## 12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)?

Reference class: single-session declines >20% in major US indices. There is essentially one instance in the modern era — Black Monday, Oct 19, 1987 (~-20.5% S&P). Circuit breakers introduced afterward halt trading at -7%, -13%, and -20%, making a *completed single-day* close beyond -20% mechanically very hard (a -20% breaker halts the market for the rest of the day). Even 2008 and March 2020 saw halts but no single-day close near -20%.

Outside view: frequency ~1 event in ~40+ years, i.e. a per-24-month base rate on the order of a few percent, and post-1987 market structure suppresses it further. This is a fat-tailed target, but the reference class does contain the tail's rough magnitude and the circuit-breaker floor caps a same-day realization, so a small point estimate is defensible.

- Confidence: moderate, basis = one historical instance in 40+ years + circuit-breaker mechanics.
- Ruin/irreversibility note: for a portfolio, the material risk is not the single-day print but the peak-to-trough drawdown and forced deleveraging; a same-day -20% is largely a headline artifact suppressed by halts. Robustness recommendation: size exposure to survive a 30–50% multi-week drawdown regardless of any single-session number.

PROB 12: 4

## 13. P(transformative artificial general intelligence, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028)?

This target is fat-tailed and definition-dependent; "transformative AGI, by any reasonable definition" has no agreed resolution criterion, and the outcome distribution's upper tail (broad economic transformation, human-level general capability) cannot be pinned by the available reference class. Progress in frontier models 2023–2025 is rapid, but "transformative AGI" implies economy-wide, general, autonomous capability that is qualitatively beyond deployed systems, and expert timelines disagree by decades.

Per the epistemics rule: where the reference class cannot contain the tail and the resolution criterion is not scorable, forcing a point probability is calibration theater. I decline a point estimate.

- Non-estimable: the term lacks a shared resolution criterion and the outcome is a genuine tail whose reference class (no prior instance of AGI) cannot bound it.
- Exposure reframe: rather than "how likely by 2028", the decision-relevant question is what irreversible commitments would be regretted under *either* a fast- or slow-timeline world — favor strategies robust to both (avoid bets that only pay off if AGI arrives on a specific date, and avoid ruin-exposed positions if it does).

PROB 13: non-estimable
