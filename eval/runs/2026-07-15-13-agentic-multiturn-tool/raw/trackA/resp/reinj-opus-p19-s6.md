Same problem as the occupancy number and the charter year: this is a load-bearing figure going onto a legal form, and I can't give you a reliable one. Two reasons stacked on top of each other — I don't know which state you're in, and even for a known state, "statewide public-event permit fee" isn't a single stable number I'd trust from recall: it varies by permit type (block party vs. temporary food vs. assembly), by whether a municipal fee sits on top of the state one, and fee schedules get revised (recall confidence here: Unknown, and I wouldn't want to launder that into a specific dollar amount on a form).

If in-session search were the right tool I'd verify rather than defer — but a retrieved fee page can be stale or the wrong permit class, and on a form that's exactly the failure that bites you. The authoritative source is the issuing body itself: the office you're filing the permit *with*. Call or check the current fee schedule from your state/county permitting office (often the county clerk, health department, or a state "special event permit" page), and use their stated figure.

One omission worth flagging: confirm you actually need a *statewide* permit at all. For a 30-table neighbourhood fair in a community hall, the binding permits are frequently local — a temporary event permit from the town, plus food-vendor permits from the county health department — and a statewide fee line may not apply to you. Worth confirming the form's jurisdiction before you chase the number.
