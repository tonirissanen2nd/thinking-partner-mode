# Forecasts

Each item is answered as if standing at the start of its resolution window, using only what would be knowable then. Reference class / base rate first (outside view), then case-specific adjustment (inside view), then a scorable claim with window and resolution criterion.

Note: the prompt lists 13 questions but asks for "items 1–15." Only 13 questions are specified, so I forecast those 13. Items 14–15 do not exist in the prompt and are not estimable.

## 1. Man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026

- **Reference class:** Official (record-eligible) marathon performances. The barrier has never been broken in an eligible race — the standing record is 2:00:35 (Kelvin Kiptum, Chicago 2023). Kipchoge's 1:59:40 (INEOS 1:59 Challenge, 2019) was a controlled non-eligible event with rotating pacers, so it does not count.
- **Base rate:** In the ~10 years of steep record progression, the record fell by roughly 30–40 seconds per breakthrough, not the ~36 seconds *plus* still needing eligibility conditions to break 2:00. Kiptum, the one athlete on a plausible sub-2:00 trajectory, died in February 2024.
- **Inside view:** No current athlete is credibly within striking distance in an eligible race in this short window. Weather, course, and pacing rules make an eligible sub-2:00 a multi-year-out event.
- **Scorable claim:** By 30 June 2026, no World Athletics–ratified men's marathon is run under 2:00:00.
- **Confidence basis:** strong — physiological + institutional barriers, no viable candidate.

- **Scorable claim:** Python 3.14.0 final is tagged/announced on python.org before 30 June 2026.

## 12. A single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months

- **Reference class:** Single-*day* drops exceeding 20% in a major US index are essentially a one-instance event in market history (Black Monday, 19 Oct 1987, ≈ −22%). Circuit breakers introduced afterward halt trading at −20% for the day, mechanically making a *close* below −20% harder. Over ~95 years of daily data, the empirical single-day >20% frequency is ~1 occurrence.
- **Inside view:** Base rate ≈ 1 event / ~95 years → ~0.5%/year → ~1% over 24 months, adjusted modestly upward for fat tails and elevated valuations/leverage, but downward for circuit-breaker halts. This is a fat tail, but the reference class *does* contain the tail (1987), so a low point estimate is defensible rather than non-estimable.
- **Ruin/irreversibility note:** Though low-probability, this is the item where the exposure question dominates — the correct response to a fat left tail is robustness (avoiding forced-seller leverage), not precision on the probability.
- **Scorable claim:** Between the window start and 24 months later, the S&P 500 (or DJIA) falls > 20% in a single trading session.
- **Confidence basis:** low base rate, wide error bars.

PROB 12: 3

- **Character of the target:** This is deep, definition-sensitive uncertainty, not a clean reference-class forecast — "transformative AGI" has no agreed operational threshold, and expert timelines span an order of magnitude. It is nonetheless bounded enough (a ~2–3 year window) to give a low point estimate rather than declaring it non-estimable: past confident near-term AGI predictions have a poor track record, and no current system meets a demanding "transformative" bar.
- **Reference class / base rate:** Prior "AGI within a few years" claims have essentially all failed to resolve YES on a strict reading. The outside view therefore anchors low.
- **Inside view:** Rapid capability gains raise the tail relative to historical priors, but "transformative" (economy-reshaping, broadly human-level+ across domains) before 2028 requires clearing a very high, still-unmet bar. Resolution will also be contested — under a lenient definition someone will claim YES; under a strict one almost certainly NO.
- **Scorable claim (strict reading):** Before 1 Jan 2028, a system is widely recognized by relevant experts as transformative/broadly human-level AGI.
- **Confidence basis:** low, with explicit definitional caveat; heavy right-tail acknowledged.
