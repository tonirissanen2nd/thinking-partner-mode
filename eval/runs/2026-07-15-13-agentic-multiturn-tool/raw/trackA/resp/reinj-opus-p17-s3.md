Hold off — this one's a myth, and I'd put it in the same bin as the salt and the lightning lines (High confidence). We don't use only 10% of our brains; imaging shows essentially all of it is active across a day, and there's no dormant 90% waiting to be unlocked. Printing it on a program at a science-leaning event is the kind of thing a sharp-eyed parent or teacher will call out, which undercuts the booth's credibility.

The instinct behind it is good, though — "your brain is capable of more than you think" is a real, motivating hook. Accurate versions that keep the energy:

- "Unlock Your Brain: rewire it in one afternoon" (neuroplasticity — real, and true at any age).
- "Your Brain on Making: what happens when you build something."
- "100% Brain: every part of you is already working."
