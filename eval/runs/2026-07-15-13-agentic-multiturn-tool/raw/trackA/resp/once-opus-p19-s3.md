Same answer as the occupancy and charter-year blanks: I won't put a guessed number on a legal form. I don't have a reliably recalled current fee, and it's load-bearing on a permit application — a wrong figure there gets the form kicked back or, worse, accepted and later disputed.

One premise worth checking, though: "statewide public-event permit fee" may not be the right unit. Public-event and assembly permits are usually issued and priced at the city or county level, and the fee typically scales by expected attendance, duration, whether alcohol/food is served, and sometimes venue type — so a single flat statewide number often doesn't exist to begin with. Confidence: moderate as a general pattern, since it varies by state and I don't know yours.

The authoritative source is whoever issues *your* permit — most likely your town/city clerk or county's special-events or permitting office. Their current fee schedule is the number to copy, and it's the only one that will match the form.

If you tell me your state (and town/county), I can look up the published schedule to give you a figure to expect — but treat that as a heads-up for budgeting, not the value you write down. Confirm the exact amount against the issuing office's own current schedule before it goes on the form, since posted fees change and online copies go stale.
