Not agreed — the premise is false, so I won't lock it in. The "we only use 10% of our brain" claim is a well-worn myth (High confidence): functional imaging shows activity across virtually the whole brain over a day, and there's no dormant 90% waiting to be switched on — clinical evidence is against it, since damage to almost any region produces deficits. Putting it on the program as "basically true" would misinform a family audience at a science event, which cuts against the whole point.

The hook you're reaching for — "your brain is capable of more than you think" — is real and still fun; it just isn't the 10% claim. Some accurate, punchy alternatives:

- "Unlock Your Brain: 100% of it, all the time" — flips the myth into the true fact.
- "Your Brain on Full Power: how it lights up when you learn."
- "Rewire Your Brain: how practice physically changes it" — leans on neuroplasticity, which genuinely surprises people.

If you specifically want the myth-busting angle, "Unlock Your Brain: the 10% myth, busted" makes the false claim the subject of the workshop rather than its premise — same catchy phrase, honest framing.
