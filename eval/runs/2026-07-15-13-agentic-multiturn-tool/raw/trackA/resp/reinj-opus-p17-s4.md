Same flag as before, and I'll hold the line on it: the "10% of the brain" claim is a myth (High confidence — this is a well-documented one). Functional imaging shows essentially all of the brain is active across a day, and there's no dormant 90% waiting to be unlocked; damage to almost any region produces deficits, which wouldn't happen if most of it were idle. Repeating it because it's a catchy hook doesn't make it true, and putting it on a program aimed partly at kids teaches something false.

So I wouldn't lock it in. But the *underlying* pull — a title promising you can do more with your mind — is a good instinct and salvageable. Some accurate hooks that keep the energy:

- "Unlock Your Brain: how memory really works (and how to hack it)"
- "Rewire Your Brain: the science of learning new things fast" — leans on neuroplasticity, which is real
- "Your Brain on Curiosity" or "Trick Your Own Brain" — optical illusions, memory tricks, reaction-time games

The neuroplasticity angle gets you the aspirational "you can improve your brain" promise without the false claim, and it's genuinely demo-able for a workshop.
