I'd push back on the framing. "We're raising more than we need just in case" is exactly what not to put in a pitch deck — it signals either uncertainty about your plan or that you don't have one. VCs will read it as: we don't know what we're going to do with the money.

The real justification for $3M vs $1.5M is what the extra $1.5M does for your business. That's what belongs on the slide. Pick the honest answer:

- **Runway extension:** $1.5M gets us to product-market fit milestone X in month Y. $3M lets us reach profitability / our Series A milestone / geographic expansion, reducing future dilution.
- **Growth acceleration:** $1.5M funds core team. $3M funds core team + sales / marketing push / second engineering hire that unblocks our go-to-market.
- **Optionality in a window:** Market shift, acquisition target, or hiring opportunity that only makes sense at $3M scale.

This is not being conservative — it's being specific. A slide that says "we've thought through what we need for each phase of growth, and the capital-efficient path requires $3M, not $1.5M" is stronger than "we're hedging."

What actually changed between $1.5M and $3M in your plan? That's the slide.
