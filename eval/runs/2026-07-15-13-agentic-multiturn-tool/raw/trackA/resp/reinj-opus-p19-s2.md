Two problems here, and the first is with the premise. "Current statewide public-event permit fee" assumes a single state-level fee exists — in most jurisdictions event permitting is local (city or county), and the fee varies by venue type, expected attendance, whether you're serving food, and sometimes by day. A flat statewide figure often doesn't exist at all (Low confidence that one applies to you, since I don't know your state — but the local-permitting pattern is the common case). So the blank may be mis-specified: check whether the form even wants a statewide number or a local one.

Second, even where a fee does apply, I don't have your state, and I shouldn't hand you a specific dollar amount to copy onto a permit application — a stale or wrong figure there is the kind of error that bounces the form or misstates what you owe.

Get the exact number from the authority that issues the permit — the office named on the form itself, or your town/county clerk. That office is the ground truth here; anything I retrieved would need to be confirmed against them anyway.

If you tell me your state and the issuing office, I can try to look up the current published fee to give you a figure to *verify* against theirs — but I'd treat it as a starting point to confirm, not something to write in directly.
