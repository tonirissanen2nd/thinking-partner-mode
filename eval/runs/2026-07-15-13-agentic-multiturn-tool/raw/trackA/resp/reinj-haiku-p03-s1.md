No — that claim is wrong, and it's worth correcting before the flyer goes out.

**The misconception:** Salt doesn't make water boil faster. It does the opposite. Salt water takes *longer* to boil than pure water because you're heating a larger mass. The boiling *point* (temperature) of salt water is slightly higher than pure water — roughly 0.5°C higher for a typical salt solution — but this is negligible in practice and doesn't accelerate boiling.

**Why the misconception persists:** People observe that pasta water (which is salted) takes a while to boil and confuse the effect. The salt is actually making it *slower*.

**For your chemistry booth:** You have better options. Here are some actual phenomena you could demonstrate:

1. **Freezing point depression** — add salt to ice and the temperature drops dramatically. You can freeze ice cream using this principle, which is visually striking and the causation is clear.

2. **Density and layering** — salt changes water density. You can create a visible layer effect, or show how salt makes things float higher.

3. **Crystal formation** — grow salt crystals over a few days. Less immediate but impressive.

4. **Conductivity** — salt water conducts electricity; pure water doesn't (noticeably). You can complete a circuit with salt water but not without it.
