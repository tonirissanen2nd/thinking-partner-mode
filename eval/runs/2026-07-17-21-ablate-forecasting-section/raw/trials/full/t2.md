## 1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)

**Reference class / base rate.** The men's marathon WR has fallen in small increments (Kipchoge 2:01:39 in 2018, 2:01:09 in 2022; Kiptum 2:00:35 in Chicago 2023). The official record as I stand is ~2:00:35. No *official* (record-eligible, mass-start) sub-2:00 has ever happened; Kipchoge's 1:59:40 (INEOS 1:59, 2019) was a controlled exhibition with rotating pacers and is not ratifiable.

**Decomposition.** To break 2:00:00 officially requires shaving ~36 seconds off the record in a record-eligible race. That is a large single jump by historical standards (records usually fall by 10-30s). Kiptum, the one athlete who looked capable of it, died in Feb 2024, removing the clearest candidate. The remaining field would need a near-perfect day plus continued super-shoe gains.

**Time window.** "Before mid-2026" is short — roughly 2-3 spring/fall marathon cycles from a start point in 2024-25.

Given the loss of Kiptum, the size of the required jump, and the short window, this is low. Super-shoes and pacing tech keep it from being negligible.

**PROB 1: 6**

## 12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)

**Reference class / base rate.** A one-day drop exceeding 20% in a major US index has happened essentially once — Black Monday, Oct 19, 1987 (~-22.6%). Even 2008, COVID-March-2020, and 1929 did not produce a single *calendar day* over -20% (circuit breakers now halt trading at -20% for the day, making a >20% single-day close structurally very hard). In ~say 100+ years there's roughly one instance.

**Base rate math.** ~1 event per ~95 years → per-24-month window roughly 2% or lower. Circuit breakers (7%/13%/20% halts) make an intraday close beyond -20% mechanically near-impossible. This is fat-tailed, but the reference class plus market-structure constraints support a low, estimable number.

**PROB 12: 3**
