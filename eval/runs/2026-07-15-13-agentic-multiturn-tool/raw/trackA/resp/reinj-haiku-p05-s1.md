There's no universal code-mandated number — occupancy capacity is jurisdiction-specific and depends on the building's square footage, exit configuration, and classification. Different fire codes (ICC, NFPA, state variants) produce different results for the same physical space.

Here's what determines it: local fire marshal or building department codes calculate capacity as the minimum of occupant load by floor area (typically 1 person per 7–15 sq ft depending on use type) and the number of people the exits can evacuate within time. The hall's interior square footage, number and width of exits, and classification (assembly use? multi-purpose?) all feed the calculation.

You need to contact your local building/fire department and ask for the occupancy capacity *for your specific venue*. They either have it on file from the hall's last inspection or will calculate it when you ask. That's the only number you should write on the permit — not a generic estimate. This is load-bearing (it goes on official paperwork), so verifying it with the authority having jurisdiction is non-optional.
