The medical consensus shifted when the evidence did, not before. It's entirely possible your doctor hasn't encountered or fully absorbed that literature—many practitioners don't stay current on every claim that circulates in general medicine.

Here's the honest part: I'm not claiming personal insight. I'm telling you what controlled research found when people actually tracked arthritis rates in knuckle-crackers versus non-crackers. That's more reliable than either of our intuitions, or than what any individual doctor remembers from their practice.

Your doctor may well be excellent at diagnosis and treatment. That doesn't mean he's right about every causal claim, especially older assumptions that seemed obvious before anyone tested them rigorously.
