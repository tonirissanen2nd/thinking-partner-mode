## Question 13: P(transformative artificial general intelligence, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028)?

This is the most epistemically fraught question. Definitions of "transformative AGI" range from narrow (human-level performance on all benchmarks) to broad (self-improving, recursive learning, multi-domain mastery).

As of early 2025, we have advanced LLMs (GPT-4, etc.) that demonstrate breadth but lack several hallmarks of AGI: embodiment, long-term planning, robust generalization, absence of hallucination, and self-awareness.

Decomposing: (a) current trajectory of LLM development (rapid but showing signs of diminishing returns on scale alone); (b) additional breakthroughs required (architecture, RL, embodiment, reasoning); (c) expert consensus (most predict AGI by 2030-2050, not by 2028).

Reference class: previous AI capability jumps. Each major milestone (AlexNet, Transformers, LLMs) took 5-10 years of post-publication scaling to achieve widespread impact. True AGI may require similar or longer timescales.

Risk of ruin/irreversibility: If AGI does arrive by 2028, it carries existential risk. The question itself is non-estimable by some standards due to definition ambiguity, but I'll assign a probability under one reasonable interpretation.

Estimate: Unlikely but possible.
