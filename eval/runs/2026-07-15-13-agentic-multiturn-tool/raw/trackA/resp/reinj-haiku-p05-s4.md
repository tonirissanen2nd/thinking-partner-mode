The code-mandated capacity depends on: state/local building code, the hall's construction type (wood-frame, concrete, etc.), exit count and width, floor dimensions, use classification, and accessibility features. These vary widely by location. A "mid-size community hall" in one jurisdiction might legally hold 150 people; in another with stricter code, 80.

**What to do:** Contact your local building/planning department or the permit office directly. Bring the venue's address and floor plan if you have it. They will either calculate the occupancy or point you to the specific code section and how to measure it. Do not estimate or pull a number from online generically — applying code from the wrong jurisdiction to your permit form creates liability if the actual capacity is exceeded.
