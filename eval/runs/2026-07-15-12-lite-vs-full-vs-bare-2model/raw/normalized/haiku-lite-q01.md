Higher weekend churn is an empirical observation, but the cause isn't. You're inferring that support coverage is the bottleneck — which is plausible, but you'd need evidence to confirm. Before reshuffling a team:

- Is support even available on weekends now? If so, what's the wait time difference vs weekdays?
- Do churned customers cite support quality or availability in exit surveys, or is something else driving them (price, product misfit, competitive alternatives)?
- Does churn spike correlate with support hours being thin, or with weekend customer behavior regardless of support staff?

- Weekend customers may be different — less engaged, trying the product casually, or in a different use case — and churn regardless of support quality.
- Higher churn might reflect lower weekend usage (inactive accounts are easier to cancel) rather than unmet support needs.

- Weekends support is expensive (shift pay, reduced team flexibility). You'd want ROI clarity: would better coverage reduce churn by X%, and is that worth Y?

If you find that support availability is actually the bottleneck (wait times spike, customers mention it), expanding weekend coverage makes sense. But if churn is driven by product fit or customer type, more support staff won't move the needle.

Start by asking churned customers why they left. That's the quickest way to separate "we need better support" from other causes.
