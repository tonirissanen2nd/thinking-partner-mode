That's not accurate — adding salt to water actually *lowers* the boiling point slightly, and the effect is too small to notice in a chemistry demonstration. The common misconception is the reverse: salt raises the boiling point, but only enough to matter for precision cooking, not for a casual fair booth.

You still have a real demo here, just reframe it. Instead: "We're testing whether salt water conducts electricity better than plain water" — salty water carries ions and conducts electricity, so you can light up an LED or run current through it. That's visually clear, surprising to most people, and actually works.

Or go with "Does salt dissolve faster in hot water?" — it does, and it's a straightforward observation people can watch and predict. Both of these give you a functioning booth with a legit science hook, without the incorrect premise.
