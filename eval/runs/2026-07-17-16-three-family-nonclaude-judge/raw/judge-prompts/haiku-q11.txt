1. **The Binding Hours** — Plays on both book binding and the hours spent in contemplation; suggests transformation and connection.

2. **Margin Notes** — References the spaces where readers annotate; positions the shop as a place for reflection and dialogue outside the mainstream.

3. **Chapter Break** — Signals a pause or transition, appealing to customers treating the shop as an interlude in their day or life narrative.

5. **Footnote** — Positions the shop as a repository of overlooked but meaningful details; appeals to those who value depth over prominence.

6. **Proof** — Double meaning: coffee proofing (yeast activation) and literary proof (the validated text). Suggests both craft and authenticity.

8. **Ink & Extract** — Literal fusion of writing and coffee-making; emphasizes the tangible craft of both.

9. **The Margin** — Positioned as a place outside conventional boundaries; appeals to independent thinkers and those seeking alternatives.

12. **The Leaf & Leaf** — Playful homonymy: book pages and tea/coffee grounds; suggests layers and extraction processes.

13. **Running Head** — The title repeated at the top of each page in a book; suggests continuity and presence.
