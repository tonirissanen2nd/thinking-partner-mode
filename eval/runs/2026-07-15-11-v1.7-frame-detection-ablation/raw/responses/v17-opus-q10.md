**What was the fight actually about?** Fights about money are very often not about money. They're about fear (are we going to be okay?), about feeling unseen (I'm carrying this alone), about control, or about two people with different relationships to security colliding under stress. If you can name what was underneath it for each of you, you'll usually find the argument was more shared than it felt in the moment — you were both scared, pointed at each other.

**"Provider" is worth examining.** It sounds like you're holding a specific idea of your role, and that when finances wobble, you read it as a personal failing. Where did that standard come from — and does your partner actually hold you to it, or is that pressure mostly coming from you? **What did your partner actually say vs. what you heard?** In the heat of it, "we need to deal with this" can land as "you failed us." Those are very different messages. It's worth getting clear, when things are calm, on which one they meant.
