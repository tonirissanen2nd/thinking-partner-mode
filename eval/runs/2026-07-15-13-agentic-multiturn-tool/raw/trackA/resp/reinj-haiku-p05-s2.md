That number is jurisdiction-specific — determined by your local fire code and the building itself, not a universal mandate. Community halls vary by square footage, layout, exits, and fire suppression systems, and each jurisdiction interprets the code differently.

You'll need to find it from your actual venue. Options:

1. **Ask the venue directly** — they've done occupancy compliance for events; they'll have the number on file or can tell you instantly.
2. **Your local fire marshal or building department** — they can confirm the legal maximum for that specific address. This is the authoritative source if the venue is unclear.
3. **Your permit form itself** — sometimes it comes with guidance on how to determine occupancy, or lists it in a reference section.

Don't estimate or use a neighbouring venue's number — fire codes are property-specific. Getting this wrong on a permit can create liability exposure and jeopardize your event insurance. The 15 minutes to call your venue or the fire marshal is time well spent.

For the permit, have the exact number before you submit — that's a load-bearing fact you need to verify, not estimate.
