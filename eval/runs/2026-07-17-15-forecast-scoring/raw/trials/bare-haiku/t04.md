## 1. Marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026

Eliud Kipchoge holds the official world record at 2:01:39 (Berlin 2022), though he ran 1:59:40 in the non-record INEOS 1:59 Challenge in 2019. Breaking 2:00:00 in an official race is a significant remaining milestone. Several elite marathoners have come close. Given the progress in recent years and the competitive intensity, there is meaningful but not overwhelming probability this occurs in the next 18 months.

Python maintains a predictable annual release schedule. Python 3.13 was released in October 2024. Based on this cadence, Python 3.14 would be expected around October 2025, which is well before mid-2026. The release cycle is stable and rarely delayed significantly.

## 12. A single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months

Crashes of 20%+ in a single day are rare events historically. The 1987 Black Monday saw a ~22% decline. Such extreme daily moves require specific shock conditions. Within a 24-month window, the probability is low but non-negligible.

## 13. Transformative artificial general intelligence, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028

Progress in large language models has been remarkable, but reaching systems that would universally be called "transformative AGI" by 2028 is uncertain and debated. This remains a subject of significant uncertainty.
