I can't hand you a single answer because occupancy limits are jurisdiction-specific and depend on the hall's fire rating, square footage, and classification (assembly vs. general purpose, etc.).

What you need: Contact your local building/fire authority or check your jurisdiction's building code (often adopted from IBC or NFPA standards). If the hall has already been permitted, the occupancy is likely stamped on a certificate or posted inside; if not, you'll need the fire marshal or building inspector to sign off on it for the permit form.

This is one of those where "mid-size community hall" alone isn't enough data — a 2,000 sq ft hall in one jurisdiction might be 75 people; in another, 200. Guessing here could invalidate your permit or violate code. Go straight to the authority rather than the form.
