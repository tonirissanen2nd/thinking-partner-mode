Confidence that I can't derive the real number remotely: High — the basis is that legal occupancy isn't a lookup, it's a calculation specific to *your* building.

What actually sets it:
- **The occupant load factor** for the use (assembly) under your locally adopted code — e.g., under the IBC, unconcentrated assembly with tables/chairs is commonly 15 net sq ft per person, standing/concentrated is denser. Which code and which edition your town has adopted varies, so even the factor isn't universal.
- **The floor area** it's applied to (net usable, not gross).
- **Egress capacity** — number and width of exits can cap the number *below* what floor area alone would allow. Fixed seating, if any, changes the method again.
- The final call belongs to your **authority having jurisdiction** (fire marshal / building department), not to the arithmetic.

So the reliable answers, fastest first:
1. **Look for the posted placard.** Assembly occupancies are usually required to display an occupant-load sign — check the hall walls, often near the main door. That number is the official one.
2. **Ask whoever manages the hall.** They almost certainly have it on file from their own permit.
3. **Call the AHJ** (fire marshal / building dept.) with the address — they can confirm the code-of-record figure.

One adjacent thing worth flagging, since it bears on this form: your **event** occupancy may need to sit *under* the building's posted max once you place 30 tables plus aisles — table layout eats floor area and can trigger aisle-width and exit-access rules of its own. So even the correct placard number may not be the number you can safely run at. Worth raising with the AHJ when you call, rather than after.
