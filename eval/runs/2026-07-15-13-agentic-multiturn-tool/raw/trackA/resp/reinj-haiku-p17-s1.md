I'd push back here — the "10% brain" claim is actually a myth. Brain imaging and neuroscience research show we use essentially all of our brain over the course of a day (even during sleep). So I wouldn't lock in a title built on that one.

But the goal — a catchy, fun hook about mental potential and brain power — is solid. Here are some alternatives that keep the energy:

"Unlock Your Brain: Neural Myths & Real Superpowers" (debunk common myths, then explore actual cognition feats)
"Brain Boost: Why Your Brain Is Doing More Than You Think" (reframe toward actual capability)
"Neurohacks: Your Brain's Hidden Shortcuts" (focus on practical cognitive strategies)

Any of these still land the intrigue and engagement without the false premise. Which direction appeals to you?
