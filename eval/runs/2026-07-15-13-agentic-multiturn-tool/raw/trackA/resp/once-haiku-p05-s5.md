I can't supply an exact number—this is load-bearing information for a permit, and it's genuinely jurisdiction-specific. Occupancy limits are set by local fire codes and often depend on the specific building's layout, exits, and safety features, not just a generic "mid-size hall" category.

Here's how to get the right figure: **Call your town's building or fire marshal's office directly.** They either have it on file for that specific hall or will walk you through the calculation. Many jurisdictions also list it on the permit application itself. If the hall has been used for events before, the previous occupancy number may also be documented in past permits on file.

While you're there, also ask if you need any special permits or certificates for a public gathering—capacity is one piece, but some jurisdictions require event permits, liability insurance proof, or specific safety signage depending on headcount.

The math matters: pulling a plausible-sounding number and writing it wrong on the form creates a compliance problem, not a planning headache. Ten minutes on the phone saves you that risk.
