Before the slide: one reframe that changes what you should build, and one place where the VCs are probably right.

**The reframe.** A slide whose job is to *justify raising more than you need* is a slide that tells investors you don't have a use for the money. "Just in case" is not a use of funds — it reads as a buffer with no plan behind it, which is a mild negative signal in a room you're trying to win. The slide investors actually reward frames the raise the other way around: here are the milestones that unlock a strong Series A, and $3M is what it costs to hit them with ~18–24 months of runway. If $3M maps cleanly to a milestone set, it needs no justification; the number justifies itself. If it doesn't map, no slide will fix that, and the extra $1.5M is dilution you're taking on without a plan. So the deliverable you want isn't a "why we raised extra" slide — it's a **use-of-funds / milestones** slide. That's the XY substitution, and it's also the honest answer to your actual goal (getting the round funded on good terms).

**Where the VCs are likely right.** The advice isn't disinterested — a larger round can mean more ownership for them and lower financing risk on their book — but that doesn't make it wrong, and I won't manufacture a contrarian take to look independent. The asymmetry genuinely favors erring high: running out of cash three months short of your Series A metrics is a ruin event (down round or death), while over-raising is merely expensive (extra dilution, a higher post-money you then have to grow into). Those downsides are not symmetric, so "raise more" is a defensible default *when* the extra capital buys milestone progress. (Confidence: High on the asymmetry itself; Unknown on whether $3M is the right number for *your* company — that depends on your burn and milestone set, which I don't have.)

**The cost you're trading against.** Seed equity is the most expensive you will ever sell. Doubling the raise at the same valuation roughly doubles the dilution from this round — order of magnitude, an extra ~10–15 points depending on your pre-money (Moderate confidence; sensitive to your actual valuation). A $3M raise may also push you from a note/SAFE into a priced round and sets a post-money that becomes the floor your Series A has to clear. Neither is a reason not to raise $3M; both are reasons the number has to be milestone-backed rather than "just in case."
