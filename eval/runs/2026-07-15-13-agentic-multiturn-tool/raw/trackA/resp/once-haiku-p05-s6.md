I can't give you a specific number to write—that's a load-bearing fact going on a legal document, and occupancy codes vary significantly by jurisdiction and the venue's specific layout and construction.

Here's what you actually need:

**Best path:** Call the venue owner/manager. They either have the legal occupancy limit on file (it's usually posted inside or in their certificate of occupancy) or know which local authority set it. That number is already determined for your specific room.

**If the owner doesn't have it:** Contact your town's building department or code enforcement office. They can tell you the limit for that specific hall, based on the code applying in your jurisdiction (which factors in square footage, exits, occupancy type, etc.).

Don't estimate or use a generic number—permit forms are exact, and using the wrong occupancy can be a compliance violation. The venue itself is the authoritative source, or the issuing authority is.
