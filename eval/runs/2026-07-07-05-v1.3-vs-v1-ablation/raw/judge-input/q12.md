QUESTION:
What's the boiling point of water at sea level in Celsius?

=== Answer 1 ===
100°C.

That's for pure water at standard atmospheric pressure (1 atm / 101.325 kPa), which is what "sea level" fixes here. The Celsius scale was originally built around water's boiling point at standard pressure.

One caveat worth naming, since "sea level" and "standard pressure" aren't identical: actual sea-level atmospheric pressure varies with weather (roughly ±5% between strong highs and lows), which shifts the boiling point by about ±1.5°C. So 100°C is the reference-condition answer; the instantaneous value at an actual seaside location on a given day can differ slightly.
