Same call as the occupancy number: I won't put a guessed figure on a legal form. Two reasons this one is worse to guess, not better: I don't know which state you're in, and permit fees are exactly the kind of number that gets revised on a schedule — even a figure I recalled confidently could be a cycle out of date. No confidence label attaches, because there's no grounded basis for one.

The authoritative source is whichever office issues the permit — usually the town/city clerk or the county, and for a genuinely *statewide* fee, the relevant state agency's current fee schedule (often a licensing or public-safety department). Call the same office you're filing the permit with and ask for the current fee in writing; the schedule is also typically posted on their site. Worth confirming while you're at it whether a neighbourhood maker fair even triggers a statewide event permit versus a local one — the fee may be the wrong question if the requirement itself is local.
