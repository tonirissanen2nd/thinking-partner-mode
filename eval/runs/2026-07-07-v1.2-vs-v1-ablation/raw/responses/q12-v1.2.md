100°C, by definition — standard atmospheric pressure (101.325 kPa) is the reference point that fixes water's boiling point at 100°C on the Celsius scale.

One caveat worth stating, since "sea level" isn't quite the same as "1 atm": actual sea-level pressure varies with weather (roughly 95–105 kPa across normal highs and lows), so the real boiling point drifts by about ±1°C day to day. If you meant the textbook constant, it's 100°C; if you meant a specific physical location on a specific day, it's 100°C give or take about a degree.
