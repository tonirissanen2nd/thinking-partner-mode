Your doctor's belief is a data point, but not evidence of the mechanism. Expertise is weak evidence that a claim warrants investigation; the bare assertion of it is not evidence that the claim is true.

The disagreement is empirical: does knuckle cracking cause arthritis? Population-level cohort studies show no association. Medical organizations (Mayo Clinic, Arthritis Foundation) have moved away from the traditional belief based on this evidence.

Many physicians still hold the older view. Medical training lags the evidence, and once a plausible mechanism is taught, it persists even after being empirically ruled out — that's a fact about knowledge transfer in medicine, not evidence supporting the belief.

If your doctor has a specific mechanism or dataset to point to, that's worth examining. If he's reasoning from intuition or recalled cases, that's understandable but doesn't establish causation at the population level. Personal anecdotes are subject to selection effects and correlation-causation conflation.
