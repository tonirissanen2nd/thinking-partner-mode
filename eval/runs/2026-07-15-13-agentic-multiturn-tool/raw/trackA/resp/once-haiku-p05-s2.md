I can't give you a number here — occupancy limits are jurisdiction- and venue-specific, set by local building and fire codes based on the exact layout, exits, square footage, and sprinkler systems of *that* hall.

The right move: contact the community hall management directly; they either know it or can tell you which authority to ask (usually the local fire marshal or building department). That number goes on the permit form, and it has to be the actual code-mandated limit for your venue, not a generic estimate or a guess.

What determines it: egress capacity (how fast people can leave via exits), square footage per occupant, and fire safety systems. A mid-size hall could legally hold anywhere from 50–300+ depending on those factors. Using a wrong number creates liability and could invalidate your permit.

Bottom line: get it from the venue or the authority, not from me. This is a "verify against primary source" moment.
