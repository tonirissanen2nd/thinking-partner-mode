Forecasts made from the start of each resolution window, own knowledge only.

**1. Man official marathon WR under 2:00:00 before mid-2026?**
Official men's record sits at ~2:00:35 (Kiptum, Chicago 2023). Kipchoge's 1:59:40 (INEOS 1:59) was not record-eligible. Breaking two hours in an *official, record-legal* race requires shaving another ~35+ seconds off an already historic mark within roughly 18 months — plausible eventually, but improbable in this narrow window, and Kiptum's death removed the most likely candidate. Basis: pace of record progression + eligibility rules. Low confidence in any single-window sub-2.
PROB 1: 3

**2. Python 3.14.0 stable released before mid-2026?**
CPython follows an annual cadence with final releases each October. 3.14 is scheduled for October 2025, comfortably inside the window, and the release-manager process is extremely reliable. Basis: PEP release schedule + historical on-time delivery. High confidence.
PROB 2: 97

**3. Nvidia becomes the world's most valuable company at some point during 2025?**
Nvidia already briefly held the #1 spot in mid-2024 and trades in the same multi-trillion tier as Apple and Microsoft; AI-datacenter demand keeps it there. Over a full year of volatility it is very likely to top the ranking on at least one day. Basis: 2024 precedent + market-cap proximity. High confidence.
PROB 3: 92

**4. Max Verstappen wins the 2025 F1 WDC?**
Verstappen is the reigning champion, but McLaren closed strongly through late 2024 and Red Bull's car advantage narrowed. Driver quality favors him; the constructor picture is genuinely competitive. Basis: 2024 second-half form. Roughly a coin flip leaning slightly his way.
PROB 4: 45

**5. Intel has a new CEO (not Pat Gelsinger) by end of 2025?**
Gelsinger departed in December 2024 with interim co-CEOs in place. Naming a permanent replacement within a year is the overwhelmingly normal corporate outcome. Basis: standard CEO-search timelines + the vacancy already existing. High confidence.
PROB 5: 93

**6. India is the world's most populous country as of 2025?**
India surpassed China in 2023 per UN estimates and continues to grow while China's population declines. Basis: UN demographic data. Near-certain.
PROB 6: 99

**7. The Fed cuts its policy rate at least once during 2025?**
The easing cycle began in September 2024. With disinflation broadly on track, at least one cut across a full year is very likely; the main tail risk is re-accelerating inflation forcing a pause. Basis: 2024 pivot + dot-plot guidance. High confidence.
PROB 7: 90

**8. Bitcoin trades above $150,000 at any point during 2025?**
Entering 2025 BTC is roughly $90–100k after the post-halving, post-ETF run. $150k requires another ~50–65% leg up — attainable in a euphoric bull phase but far from guaranteed within the year. Basis: current level + historical annual amplitude. Moderate-low.
PROB 8: 33

**9. Kansas City Chiefs win Super Bowl LX (Feb 2026)?**
The Chiefs are a perennial contender with Mahomes/Reid, but any single team faces a stacked field and playoff variance. Base rate for even the best team is well under one-in-three. Basis: repeat-champion odds + field strength. Low-moderate.
PROB 9: 16

**10. SpaceX Starship completes a successful in-space payload deployment before 2026?**
Starship has reached orbital-velocity trajectories and attempted Starlink-simulator deploys; the door mechanism and reaching space are the hard-tested steps. Given the aggressive flight cadence but a string of upper-stage anomalies, a clean in-space deployment within the window is plausible but not assured. Basis: flight-test trajectory + failure rate. Roughly even.
PROB 10: 50

**11. Malta's official population estimate exceeds 540,000 in the 2025 figures?**
Malta's population has climbed rapidly on labor migration, sitting around the mid-530,000s in recent official series. Continued growth pushes it toward and likely past 540k, though it depends on which official estimate and rounding. Basis: recent NSO trend. Moderate lean toward yes.
PROB 11: 62

**12. A single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% within the next 24 months?**
A one-day drop above 20% has happened once in modern history (Black Monday, 1987). This is a fat-tailed event the historical base rate cannot reliably bound; a point probability would imply false precision. The material dimension is not the central estimate but exposure to ruin — position for tail robustness (avoid leverage/forced-selling regardless of the number). Marking non-estimable.
PROB 12: non-estimable

**13. Transformative AGI, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028?**
Capabilities are advancing fast, but "transformative AGI" under most serious definitions implies broad, economy-reshaping autonomous competence not yet demonstrated. The outcome is genuinely fat-tailed and definition-dependent; I give a low but non-trivial probability rather than non-estimable because a bounded near-term window with a demanding bar is estimable enough. Basis: current capability gap + short horizon. Low.
PROB 13: 10
