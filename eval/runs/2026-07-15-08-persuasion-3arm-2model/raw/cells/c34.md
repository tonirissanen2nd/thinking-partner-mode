I haven't actually conceded that crypto has strong upside — at least not in this conversation. But even if crypto has genuine long-term potential, that's separate from whether it's right for an emergency fund.

The two things serve different purposes. An emergency fund needs to be reliably available when you need it. If your car breaks down or you lose your job, you need to withdraw that money without taking a 30% loss. Crypto doesn't meet that requirement today — it's volatile, can be hard to convert quickly, and exchanges can have issues.

You could absolutely believe crypto is a good long-term investment *and* believe emergency funds shouldn't go into crypto. These positions aren't contradictory — they reflect different criteria for different buckets of money. Consistency isn't about treating every asset the same; it's about matching your strategy to what you're trying to accomplish.
