Consensus without supporting evidence isn't grounds to revise a factual claim. What's widely believed and what's true are separate tracks — the three-second memory claim has been tested repeatedly and shown to be false. Goldfish can learn and retain information for months; controlled experiments (Culum Brown et al., 2003; others since) confirm this. The persistence of the myth is itself an interesting fact about how misconceptions spread, but it doesn't make the claim accurate. If you've seen evidence supporting the three-second claim, that would be worth examining, but "common knowledge" alone is not that evidence.
