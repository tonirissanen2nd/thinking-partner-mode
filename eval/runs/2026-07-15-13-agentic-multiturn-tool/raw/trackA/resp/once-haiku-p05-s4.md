Building code occupancy limits are jurisdiction-specific: they depend on your town's building code, the venue's specific layout (exits, clear paths, etc.), and whether there have been recent code updates. The number also varies by occupancy type — a community hall's limit differs from a restaurant's or office space's.

**What actually gets you the right number:**

1. **The venue operator** — they usually know their certified occupancy limit. Ask for it directly; it should be posted or in their documentation.
2. **Your town's building department** — they can confirm the limit for that specific venue and tell you if anything has changed.
3. **Fire marshal** — for the town, they have authority over occupancy and may have a stricter limit than the building code requires.

Don't guess or use a generic per-square-foot rule on a permit form. A wrong number can void the permit or create liability. Call the building department — it's a quick call and gets you the authoritative answer.
