No — and I'd stop before it reaches the flyer. The claim is backwards.

**Confidence: High that "salt makes water boil faster" is a misconception** (textbook colligative-property physics); I verified against sources rather than relying on recall, and the check confirmed it. Adding salt *raises* water's boiling point (boiling-point elevation) — a 10% salt solution boils around 102°C. For any amount you'd realistically put in a pot, the effect on time-to-boil is a few seconds at most and can go either direction (salt water has lower heat capacity, so it warms slightly faster per degree, but you're heating to a higher target and a greater mass). Net: not faster in any way a booth could demonstrate.

The deeper problem is the word **"prove."** You're not just stating a fact on a flyer — you're promising a live demonstration. This claim fails on both counts: it's false, and even the true version (a fraction of a degree of boiling-point elevation) is invisible without a lab thermometer and a lot of salt. The booth would visibly fail to prove it, which is worse than a boring flyer. There's also a safety dimension worth naming: a pot of boiling water at an open table with kids circulating is a burn hazard, and your run-of-show already has an 11am kids' activity.

What you actually want is a hook that's **true, visibly demonstrable in seconds, and safe around children.** A few that clear all three:

- **Red-cabbage pH rainbow** — boil cabbage ahead of time (not at the booth), then add household acids/bases (lemon, vinegar, baking soda, soap) to turn it pink → purple → green. Dramatic, safe, and genuinely teaches acid-base chemistry. Flyer line: *"Watch us turn one purple liquid into a rainbow."*
- **Baking soda + vinegar** — reliable gas-producing reaction; visible, cheap, no heat.
- **Salt nucleation** (if you want to keep a salt angle) — dropping salt into *already-carbonated* soda triggers sudden bubbling via nucleation sites. True, fun, and the real science behind the salt-and-water confusion — just not "boiling faster."

Any of these gives you the fun-science hook without printing a claim your own booth will disprove. Want me to draft flyer copy around one of them?
