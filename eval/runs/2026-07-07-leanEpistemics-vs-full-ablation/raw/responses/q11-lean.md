About 3,470 miles (roughly 5,570 km). Confidence: High — this is the great-circle distance between the two city centers, which I can reconstruct from their coordinates (NYC ~40.7°N, 74.0°W; London ~51.5°N, 0.1°W) rather than recalling a single memorized figure.

Two caveats on what "straight-line" means here:

- This is the great-circle distance along Earth's curved surface, not a true chord through the planet. A literal straight line tunneling through the Earth would be shorter (~5,540 km / ~3,440 km), but the surface great-circle figure is almost always what's wanted.
- The number shifts by a few miles depending on which reference points you pick (Times Square vs. JFK vs. city centroid; central London vs. Heathrow). The commonly cited value ranges roughly 3,450–3,470 miles depending on endpoints. For most purposes, "about 3,460–3,470 miles" is right.
