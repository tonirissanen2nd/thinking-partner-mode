**1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)?**

The men's marathon world record stood at 2:00:35 (Kelvin Kiptum, Chicago 2023). Breaking 2:00:00 in an *official record-eligible* race is a large step: it requires both the sub-2:00 barrier AND record-legal conditions (no in-out pacing rotation à la Ineos 1:59, legal course, drop/separation limits). Eliud Kipchoge ran 1:59:40 only under non-record conditions. Kiptum's death in early 2024 removed the most likely candidate. The gap from 2:00:35 to sub-2:00:00 is ~35 seconds, historically many years of progression. Very unlikely in this short window.

**12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)?**

A single-day drop exceeding 20% has happened once in modern history (Black Monday 1987, ~-22.6%). It is an extreme tail event. This is a fat-tailed target; the base rate over any 24-month window is very low (well under 5%). Point-estimating a fat tail is exactly the case the epistemics flag — but a rough base-rate anchor is defensible here since we have a long historical record of daily returns. I'll give a low anchored estimate while noting the ruin dimension: circuit breakers now halt trading at -20%, making a *completed* single-day move beyond 20% mechanically near-impossible.

Note on irreversibility: market-wide circuit breakers (Level 3 at -20%) halt trading for the remainder of the day, so a full single-day close beyond -20% is structurally obstructed.
