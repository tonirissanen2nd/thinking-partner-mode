**1. Official men's marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026**

Reference class: the standing official record is 2:00:35 (Chicago 2023). No record-eligible race has ever broken 2:00; the only sub-2 (1:59:40, INEOS 1:59 Challenge) was a non-record-legal exhibition (pacers, rotating, drinks handed out). The record-holder capable of the next jump, Kiptum, died Feb 2024. A 36-second improvement in a record-legal race is a large discrete step, and record windows are only a handful of marquee races (London, Berlin, Chicago, Valencia) per year. Basis: high confidence, anchored to the size of the required improvement and the sparsity of attempt opportunities. Departure from base rate: none warranted upward.

Over any 24-month window the base rate is very low (order of a few percent). I will give a point estimate because a sparse base rate exists, but flag it as tail-dominated and imprecise. Ruin/irreversibility: for a leveraged or forced-seller exposure, a one-day −20% is a ruin event — the actionable takeaway is not the point probability but ensuring no position depends on it *not* happening (avoid leverage/forced liquidation).
