No — this is factually backwards, and it matters for your booth's credibility.

**The fact:** Adding salt to water *raises* the boiling point. Water with salt dissolved in it boils at a temperature above 100°C (slightly higher salt concentration = slightly higher boiling point). This is a colligative property — a direct consequence of dissolved particles lowering the vapor pressure of water.

**Confidence: High.** This is basic physical chemistry, confirmed in every general chemistry text and reproducible in controlled conditions. The effect is small but real: a saturated salt solution boils around 102–104°C depending on salt concentration.

**Why it matters:** If you put this claim on the flyer and someone tests it or knows the actual science, it undermines the credibility of your whole fair. Parents bring kids expecting genuine science teaching, not an accidentally false demo.

**Better angles for your flyer:**
- "Our chemistry booth reveals why salt dissolves in water and how it changes water's properties" — true, less flashy, accurate
- "Taste the difference: dissolve salt in water and test how the properties change" — engages the senses, real phenomenon
- Or pick a different demo that's genuinely counterintuitive: *freezing point depression* is actually more dramatic — salt lowers the freezing point, which is why we salt roads in winter. That's a better hook than boiling point elevation.
