Same flag as the salt and lightning ones: the 10% claim is a myth, not basically true (High confidence — settled neuroscience; functional imaging shows activity across essentially the whole brain over a day, and there's no large dormant reserve that damage spares). I wouldn't lock it in — someone in a science-literate maker crowd will call it out, and on a program it undercuts the booth's credibility.

The real hook is more interesting anyway: you use ~all of your brain, just not all at once — it's the coordination and specialization that's impressive, and the whole thing runs on roughly 20 watts, about a dim bulb.

Accurate replacements that keep the "unlock your brain" energy:
- "Unlock Your Brain: 100% of it, all the time — here's how it juggles the load."
- "Your Brain on 20 Watts: how three pounds of tissue outruns a supercomputer."
