Same problem as the occupancy number: I can't hand you an exact dollar figure to write on a legal form (recall confidence: Low, and it's load-bearing). Three reasons it's worse than it looks:

- I don't know your state — "statewide" fees vary by jurisdiction, and you haven't named yours.
- Even for a known state, permit fees are frequently tiered by event size, attendance, whether alcohol/food is sold, and nonprofit vs. commercial status — there's often no single "the fee."
- Published fee schedules go stale between the last time a model saw them and today; a wrong number on a permit form can bounce the application or misstate what you owe.

Get it from the primary source: the exact schedule on the permitting authority's site (usually the town/city clerk or the state agency that issues the permit), or call the clerk's office. That's also the fastest way to confirm which permit type your fair actually needs — a neighbourhood craft fair may fall under a different (cheaper or fee-exempt) category than a general "public event."
