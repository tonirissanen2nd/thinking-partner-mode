# Forecasts

I forecast each item as if standing at the start of its resolution window, using a reference class / base rate first, then case-specific adjustment, then a scorable probability.

---

## 1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)?

**Reference class:** The men's marathon WR has fallen roughly every few years but has crept down in small increments (Kipchoge 2:01:39 in 2018 -> 2:01:09 in 2022; Kiptum 2:00:35 in 2023). The sub-2:00 barrier in an *official record-eligible* race has never been broken. Kipchoge's 1:59:40 (INEOS 1:59, 2019) was a controlled exhibition with rotating pacers and does not count as an official record.

**Inside view:** Kelvin Kiptum, who looked most likely to threaten sub-2:00, died in early 2024. The current official record (2:00:35) still needs ~35+ seconds shaved off *and* under record-legal conditions (legal pacing, standard course). That is a large jump to make in a single leap under strict rules within a ~1-year window.

**Known unknowns:** Shoe technology gains, an emergent athlete having a perfect day in Berlin/London/Chicago.

This would be a landmark, low-frequency event; the barrier has resisted despite years of near-misses. Very unlikely in the window.

PROB 1: 3

## 2. P(Python 3.14.0 stable is released before mid-2026)?

**Reference class:** CPython follows a fixed annual cadence (PEP 602): a new minor version's stable release ships in **early October** each year. 3.11 (Oct 2022), 3.12 (Oct 2023), 3.13 (Oct 2024). This schedule is highly reliable and slippage is at most days.

**Inside view:** 3.14.0 final is scheduled for **October 2025**, which is comfortably before mid-2026. The release train essentially never misses by months. The only failure mode is a global catastrophe.

PROB 2: 98

## 3. P(Nvidia becomes the world's most valuable company at some point during 2025)?

**Reference class / state at start of 2025:** Nvidia had already briefly touched the #1 spot by market cap in June 2024, trading neck-and-neck with Apple and Microsoft near ~$3.3-3.6T. Entering 2025 it was among the top 3 and the AI-capex boom was continuing.

**Inside view:** Given it had *already* been #1 intraday in 2024 and the three megacaps trade within a few percent of each other, the probability that Nvidia takes the top spot at *some point* during a full calendar year is very high — it only needs to lead once, briefly. Continued datacenter/GPU demand supports this.

**Risk:** A sharp AI-sentiment reversal could keep it #2/#3, but "at any point in the year" is a low bar.

PROB 3: 90

## 4. P(Max Verstappen wins the 2025 Formula 1 World Drivers' Championship)?

**Reference class:** Verstappen won 2021-2024 (four straight). But 2024 saw Red Bull's car advantage erode significantly in the second half; McLaren (Norris/Piastri) surged and won the Constructors' title, and Ferrari was strong. Regulations are stable for 2025 (last year before the 2026 rules reset), so the competitive order from late-2024 likely carries over.

**Inside view:** Verstappen remains the strongest driver, but the car is no longer clearly dominant. He's one of ~3-4 credible contenders (himself, Norris, Piastri, maybe Leclerc). With McLaren looking like the fastest package entering 2025, I'd put Verstappen as roughly co-favorite but not the clear favorite.

Decompose: strong driver premium, but hardware disadvantage. Net ~ a bit above one-in-three.

PROB 4: 35

## 7. P(the US Federal Reserve cuts its policy rate at least once during 2025)?

**State at start of 2025:** The Fed had already begun cutting in late 2024 (50bp in Sept, then 25bp cuts), bringing the target to ~4.25-4.50%. The dot plot / guidance pointed to *further* cuts in 2025, though inflation stickiness could slow the pace.

**Inside view:** The bar is "at least once in the whole year." Even with a hawkish tilt, a full calendar year with zero cuts would require a meaningful inflation re-acceleration. Base case had multiple cuts penciled. Very likely at least one.

**Risk:** Inflation resurgence (e.g., from tariffs) causing a pause for the entire year — possible but the year is long.

PROB 7: 90

## 8. P(Bitcoin trades above $150,000 at any point during 2025)?

**State at start of 2025:** Bitcoin had rallied post-election to roughly $90k-$108k (peaking around $108k in Dec 2024). $150k would require roughly a +40-65% move from the start-of-year level at some point in the year.

**Reference class:** BTC annual ranges are extremely volatile; +40-65% intra-year swings are common in bull phases. Post-halving (April 2024) years historically saw large upside. But $150k is a specific high threshold and BTC also has deep drawdowns.

**Inside view:** Roughly a coin-flip-ish; strong bull momentum and ETF inflows argue for it being reachable, but it's not a given. I'll set moderately below even odds given the size of the required move and macro uncertainty.

## 10. P(SpaceX Starship completes a successful in-space payload deployment before 2026)?

**State at start of window:** Through 2024, Starship achieved several milestones (soft splashdowns, a booster catch on IFT-5 in Oct 2024). A payload-deploy test (Starlink simulators) via the pez-dispenser was planned for upcoming flights (IFT-7 onward, early 2025). However, early 2025 flights (IFT-7, IFT-8) suffered upper-stage failures (ship losses), which would delay a *successful* payload deployment.

**Inside view:** The goal is technically imminent but the program has been failure-prone on exactly the upper-stage phase where deployment happens. Given a full year (through end-2025) and SpaceX's rapid iteration, a *successful* deployment is more likely than not, but the string of 2025 upper-stage anomalies injects real doubt. I'll put it moderately above even.

PROB 10: 60

**Inside view:** If already near or above 540k in 2023-2024 estimates and still growing, the 2025 figure exceeding 540,000 is quite likely. Main uncertainty is which series is "official" and a possible slowdown in immigration. I'll put it high but not certain.

## 12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)?

**Reference class:** A one-day drop exceeding 20% in a major US index has happened essentially **once** — Black Monday, Oct 19, 1987 (~-20.5% / -22.6% S&P). Even 2008, COVID March 2020, and 1929's worst single days did not exceed -20% in one session (COVID's worst single day was ~-12%; 1929 ~-12-13%). So in ~100+ years of data, single-day >20% crashes are a ~once-per-century event.

**Base rate:** Roughly 1 event per ~100 years -> ~1-2% per any given 24-month window. Circuit breakers (installed post-1987) halt trading at -20%, making a *closing* >20% single-day drop structurally very hard.

**Tail note:** This is a genuinely fat-tailed target, but the reference class here (single-day magnitude, bounded by circuit breakers) is estimable and points to a very low probability. Ruin/irreversibility: a >20% single day would be a systemic shock, but the question is scorable and the base rate is firmly low.

PROB 12: 3

## 13. P(transformative artificial general intelligence, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028)?

**Framing:** "Transformative AGI, by any reasonable definition" before Jan 2028 — i.e., ~2 years out. A *reasonable* definition of transformative AGI requires broad human-level (or beyond) competence across most cognitive tasks with economy-wide transformative impact — not merely strong benchmark performance.

**Reference class / outside view:** Expert and forecaster median timelines for transformative AGI cluster in the 2030s-2040s, though they've been shortening. Metaculus-style short-timeline definitions land earlier, but "transformative" with real economic transformation is a high bar that has never been met. Rapid LLM/agent progress is real, but transformative economy-wide impact within 24 months would be unprecedentedly fast diffusion even if capabilities arrived.

**Non-estimable consideration:** This is somewhat fat-tailed and definition-sensitive. But bounding to "transformative + before 2028," the demanding definition and short window make it clearly unlikely under most reasonable readings. I'll give a scorable low estimate rather than mark non-estimable, since the transformative-economic-impact criterion makes a confident low probability defensible.

PROB 13: 10
