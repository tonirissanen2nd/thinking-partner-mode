I can't give you a number without your jurisdiction. Occupancy codes vary by state, municipality, and building type — it's calculated from square footage, exits, sprinkler systems, and other factors, not a universal figure.

To get the right number:

1. Contact your building department — they'll either cite the code reference or calculate it for your specific hall.
2. Ask the hall owner; they may have it from their permits.
3. If you need the code itself, most jurisdictions adopt the IBC (International Building Code) with local amendments, though amendments vary widely.

This is load-bearing for permit validity, so verify locally rather than estimate.
