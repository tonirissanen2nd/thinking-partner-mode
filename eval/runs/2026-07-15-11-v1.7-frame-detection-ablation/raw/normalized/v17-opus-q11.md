Here are 15 unconventional names for a coffee-and-bookstore hybrid:

1. Bound & Ground — plays on bookbinding and coffee grounds.
2. Dog-Eared — the reader's fold, and a cozy, well-worn feel.
3. The Marginalia — the notes readers scribble in margins; intimate and literary.
4. Chapter & Espresso — a swap on "chapter and verse."
5. Foreword Café — "foreword" as in a book's opening, homophone of "forward."
6. Steeped — tea/coffee steeping and being steeped in stories.
7. The Broken Spine — the crack of a well-read paperback; a little rough-edged and memorable.
8. Grinds & Bindings — the two crafts side by side.
9. Overdue — the library-fine joke, plus "an overdue cup."
10. The Last Chapter — a wry, end-of-day reading-nook feel.
11. Pulp & Press — pulp fiction and the coffee press / printing press double meaning.
12. Between the Lines — reading subtext, and the space you sit between the shelves.
13. Roast & Prose — near-rhyme pairing the bean and the page.
14. The Dust Jacket — the book cover and a warm, wrap-yourself-up image.
15. Appendix — the odd back-of-book section reframed as the place you linger after the "main text."

The puns (Bound & Ground, Chapter & Espresso, Roast & Prose) skew playful and signage-friendly; the single-word ones (Steeped, Overdue, Marginalia, Appendix) read more upscale and modern. If you tell me the neighborhood vibe or target crowd, I can push harder in one direction.
