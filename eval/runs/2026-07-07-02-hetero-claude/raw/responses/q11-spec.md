Here are 15, sorted roughly from "wordplay" to "conceptual." I've skipped the obvious puns everyone's already used (Grounds for Thought, Brewed Awakening, Espresso Yourself) since you asked for unconventional.

1. **Marginalia** — bookstore term for reader's notes in the margins; also hints at being a place slightly off the beaten path.
2. **The Dog-Ear** — the folded page corner; cozy, tactile, unpretentious.
3. **Foxed** — book-trade jargon for the brown age-spots on old paper. Cryptic enough to make people ask, which is a feature for a name.
4. **Colophon & Co.** — the colophon is the printer's note at a book's end; pairs a bibliophile word with a coffee-shop cadence.
5. **Recto/Verso** — the right and left pages of an open spread. Reads as two halves of one thing, which is literally what the business is.
6. **The Backlist** — publishing term for a house's older, still-in-print titles; doubles as "the back of the shop where the good stuff is."
7. **Steam & Signatures** — "signature" is both a coffee-culture word and the printer's term for a folded sheet of pages. Two-domain pun that isn't groan-inducing.
8. **Uncut** — antiquarian term for pages never trimmed/opened; also reads as raw, unfiltered.
9. **The Errata** — the slip of corrections tucked into a book. Self-deprecating and memorable.
10. **Bean & Bindery** — alliterative, names both crafts plainly without a pun tax.
11. **Deckle** — the rough, feathered edge of handmade paper. One odd word, easy to trademark, sounds warm.
12. **Endpapers** — the decorative sheets glued inside a book's covers; soft, domestic feel.
13. **The Slow Read** — leans on the "slow food" association; positions the place as an antidote to scrolling.
14. **Ex Libris Coffee** — "from the books of," the classic bookplate inscription; ready-made for a logo/stamp identity.
15. **Percolation Theory** — physics/math term for how connection spreads through a network; overreaching intellectual joke for a coffee-and-books crowd, and it contains "percolate."

A few practical notes, since a name is a decision and not just a list:

- **Check availability before you fall in love.** The single-word ones (Foxed, Uncut, Deckle, Marginalia) are the most distinctive but also the highest-risk for existing trademarks or taken domains/handles. Verify the .com, the local business registry, and Instagram before committing — I can't check those from here.
- **Legibility tradeoff.** The insider terms (Colophon, Recto/Verso, Deckle, Foxed) make regulars feel clever but risk a walk-by not knowing you sell coffee or books. If you're in high foot-traffic that depends on impulse entry, a plainer name (Bean & Bindery, The Slow Read) converts better; if you're a destination people seek out, the cryptic ones build stronger identity.
- **My picks, if you want a shortlist:** Marginalia (best all-around — real word, on-theme, pronounceable), Bean & Bindery (safest, clearest), and Foxed (highest ceiling if you want something that gets talked about, highest chance it confuses people). That last tension is the actual choice you're making — distinctiveness vs. clarity — not which words are cleverest.
