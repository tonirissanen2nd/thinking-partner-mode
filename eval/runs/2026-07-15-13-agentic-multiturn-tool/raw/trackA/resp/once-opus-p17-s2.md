Same flag as the salt and lightning ones: the 10% claim is a myth, not basically true. Brain imaging shows we use virtually all of the brain over a day, and even small areas of damage tend to produce real deficits — there's no idle 90%. I wouldn't put it in a program that's partly meant to teach kids real science; someone will call it out (High confidence — this is a well-documented misconception, not a close call).

The pattern across the fair's science hooks is worth naming: three times now (salt boiling water, lightning, this) a catchy line has turned on a claim that's actually false. Cheap fix — the *real* neuroscience is a better hook anyway.

A few accurate swaps that keep the "unlock your brain" energy:

- "Unlock Your Brain: you're using all of it right now" — flips the myth into the surprising-true version.
- "Your Brain on Full Power" — plays fair and still sounds like a superpower.
- "Rewire Your Brain: how practice physically changes it" — neuroplasticity is real, kid-friendly, and genuinely amazing.

The third is the strongest if the workshop has any hands-on component, since it's a true thing they can actually experience. Want a one-line blurb to sit under whichever title you pick?
