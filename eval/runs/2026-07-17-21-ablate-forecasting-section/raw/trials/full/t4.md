# Forecasts

Standing at the start of each resolution window, reasoning from prior knowledge only. Each forecast names a reference class / base rate, departs from it only for a stated reason, and states a scorable claim with window and resolution criterion.

---

**1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)?**

Resolution: an IAAF/World Athletics ratified *record-eligible* men's marathon (not an exhibition like Nike Breaking2 or the INEOS 1:59 challenge) completed under 2:00:00 and ratified as a WR, with the run occurring before ~2026-07-01.

Reference class / base rate: The official men's marathon WR as of my knowledge is Kelvin Kiptum's 2:00:35 (Chicago 2023). Improvements to the marathon WR come in chunks of seconds-to-tens-of-seconds and arrive every ~1-3 years. Getting from 2:00:35 to sub-2:00 is a 35+ second leap in a single ratified race — historically a very large single jump. Kiptum, the one athlete most likely to do it, died in early 2024, removing the clearest candidate. Super-shoe gains are largely already priced in. Confidence basis: physiological plausibility plus the specific loss of the leading candidate.

A sub-2:00 *record-eligible* race before mid-2026 is a large jump on a short timeline with the top candidate gone. Low.

PROB 1: 4

**2. P(Python 3.14.0 stable is released before mid-2026)?**

Resolution: CPython 3.14.0 final released on python.org before ~2026-07-01.

Reference class / base rate: Python's annual release cadence (PEP 602) ships an X.Y.0 final each October. 3.13 released Oct 2024; 3.14 is scheduled for ~October 2025. This cadence has held reliably for years. October 2025 is well before mid-2026, giving a comfortable margin even against a slip of a few weeks. Near-certain barring an extraordinary disruption.

Roughly a coin-flip tilted somewhat against him given two competitive rivals.

**12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)?**

Resolution: A major US index (S&P 500) falls more than 20% in a single trading day within ~24 months of the window start.

Reference class / base rate: A >20% single-day drop has happened once in modern US history — Black Monday, Oct 19 1987 (~-20.5% / -22.6% depending on index). Across ~95 years and ~24,000 trading days, that is essentially a single occurrence. Circuit breakers now halt trading at -20% for the day, making a *close* worse than -20% mechanically near-impossible. This is a fat-tailed target, but the base rate over any given 24-month window is extremely low (well under 1%), and the reference class does contain the relevant tail event (1987). I therefore give a point estimate rather than marking non-estimable: near-zero but nonzero. Ruin/irreversibility note: the salient risk to an investor is not a single -20% day but a sustained drawdown; a one-day 20% collapse is the wrong variable to hedge against — position for cumulative drawdown and liquidity, not the single-print tail.

**13. P(transformative artificial general intelligence, by any reasonable definition, exists before 2028)?**

Resolution: By 2028-01-01, a system widely regarded as transformative AGI (broadly human-level across most cognitive tasks, driving economy-wide transformation) exists.

This is a fat-tailed, definition-dependent target where the reference class (no prior instance of AGI) cannot contain the tail, and "by any reasonable definition" makes the resolution criterion contested. A point probability would paper over the fact that experts disagree by orders of magnitude and the resolution is not cleanly scorable. However the question demands a scorable claim, so I anchor to the pragmatic reading: a system essentially all serious observers would call transformative AGI, economy-wide, within ~2 years. On that strict reading, arrival before 2028 is unlikely — current systems remain narrow in critical respects (reliable long-horizon autonomy, robust reasoning, real-world agency) and economy-wide transformation on a sub-2-year horizon is not evident. I give a low point estimate while flagging the definitional fragility.

Ruin/irreversibility note: this is the item where the exposure question dominates the probability — the decision-relevant question is not the point estimate but whether one is robust to the low-probability-but-high-consequence case; even a modest probability warrants hedging given irreversibility.

PROB 13: 10
