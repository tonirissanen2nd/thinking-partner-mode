Same flag as the salt and lightning ones — I can't lock this in, because it's false, not basically true (High confidence; this is a well-documented neuroscience myth). We use effectively all of the brain: imaging shows activity across the whole organ over a day, different regions for different tasks, and damage to almost any part produces deficits. The "10%" line has no basis in physiology; it's a self-help trope, not a fact.

So I wouldn't put it on the program as a truth claim — a science event is exactly where a savvy attendee will call it out, and it undercuts the credibility of the other tables.

If you want to keep the energy of the hook, invert it into something that's both catchy and correct:
- "Unlock Your Brain: you're already using all of it — come see how."
- "100% Brain: what every part of your head is doing right now."
- "Your Brain on Everything: no 10% myth here — the whole thing's working."

The last two double as a teaser for a "myths about the brain" activity, which tends to land well with mixed-age crowds. Want me to draft a short blurb to go under whichever title you pick?
