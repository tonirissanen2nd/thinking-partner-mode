# Forecasts

Standing at the start of each resolution window, using prior knowledge only. Each item: reference class / base rate first (outside view), then case-specific adjustment (inside view), then a scorable probability.

**1. P(a man sets an official marathon world record under 2:00:00 before mid-2026)?**

Reference class: ratified IAAF/World Athletics marathon records in record-eligible races. The standing official record is ~2:00:35 (set Oct 2023). Sub-2:00 has only ever been run in a non-record-eligible exhibition (paced, rotating pacers, closed course) — never under record conditions. Progression of the official record has been ~20–30 s per breakthrough, roughly once every 1–3 years, and the leading candidate to attack it died in early 2024. To go from 2:00:35 to sub-2:00:00 official requires a ~35+ s leap in a legal race within a ~1-year window — well beyond the historical per-cycle step. Base rate of a single record attempt clearing that specific 35 s gap in one window: very low, and no near-term staged attempt is scheduled under legal conditions.

Confidence basis: physiological-progression + event-calendar. Estimable (not a tail event — bounded human performance).

**12. P(a single-day US stock-market crash exceeding 20% occurs within the next 24 months)?**

Reference class: single-session declines of >20% in major US indices. In the entire history of US markets this has essentially happened once (Black Monday, Oct 1987, ~-20.5% S&P); even 2008 and March 2020 had their worst single days near -9% to -12%, blunted further by modern trading halts (the -20% level triggers a market-wide circuit-breaker halt for the day). So the empirical base rate for a *single-day* >20% drop in any given 24-month window is on the order of once in many decades, and current market structure actively suppresses it.

This is a fat-tailed target, but here the tail cuts toward *lower* probability of the specific single-day threshold, and the reference class does contain the relevant precedent — so a low point estimate is defensible rather than misleading. Ruin/irreversibility note: the material risk is not this precise one-day figure but a large multi-day drawdown; exposure management (diversification, avoiding leverage) should not hinge on whether the loss arrives in one session or five.

Confidence basis: century-scale single-session record + circuit-breaker structure. Estimable at the low end.
