Reference class: men's marathon world records. The current record stands at 2:01:09 (Kipchoge, 2022). A sub-2:00:00 represents a ~70-second improvement, which would be the largest single-jump in modern marathon history. WRs improve gradually; jumps of this magnitude occur rarely—perhaps once per decade in the modern era. The time window (Feb 2025 to mid-2026) is roughly 16 months. Given Kipchoge is aging, other elite marathoners would need to step up. While not impossible (several runners are within striking distance), the combination of the gap size and limited time makes this a low-probability event. Base rate for "record broken in any given year" is ~20%; conditioning on this being a sub-2:00 specifically reduces it.
