No — that's backwards. Salt raises the boiling point of water slightly (to ~100.5°C for a typical saltwater solution), not lowers it. Water with salt in it boils *hotter*, not faster. This is a load-bearing misstatement to catch before print.

The confusion likely stems from salt's role in *ice cream making* — salt lowers the freezing point, which is the opposite direction. Common enough error that it's worth reframing.

For a chemistry booth with actual impact, consider: (1) observing the boiling-point elevation directly with a thermometer; (2) crystallization — grow salt crystals visibly in real time; (3) density layers — salt water sinks in fresh water, striking visual; (4) freezing-point depression with ice and salt, where salt *does* lower the temperature dramatically. Any of these work better than disproving a claim on the flyer.
