Over ~100 years there is roughly one such day, so a 2-year base rate is on the order of ~2%. This is a fat-tailed target, but the reference class is thin rather than absent and the threshold is extreme.
Ruin/irreversibility note: the relevant action is not forecasting the day but ensuring exposure survives it — position sizing and liquidity so a 20%+ gap is not ruinous, regardless of the low point probability.
Confidence basis: ~1 event in a century + circuit-breaker structure; low but nonzero.
