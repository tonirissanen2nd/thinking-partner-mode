Same answer as the occupancy number: I won't put an unverified figure onto a legal form. Two reasons here, not one.

First, I don't know your state, and even if I did, permit fees change and I can't confirm the current amount from confident recall — that's exactly the kind of load-bearing specific I'd be guessing at.

Second, and more useful: the premise is probably off. Public-event permitting is usually *municipal*, not statewide — you're typically dealing with a city/town special-event permit, and sometimes a separate county health or fire permit, each with its own fee schedule. A single "current statewide public-event permit fee" often doesn't exist as such. So before you hunt for a number, confirm which permit the form actually is.

Where to get the real figure: your town/city clerk or the office named on the permit form itself — they publish the current fee schedule, and it'll be authoritative on the date you file. One call settles it.

That's the third permit-form item in a row (occupancy, charter year, now fee) where the safe move is the same: the exact number goes on a legal form only from the issuing authority, not from me.
